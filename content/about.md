## About Me

I am a Master's student in Computing Science (Thesis) at the **University of Alberta**, working under the supervision of [Dr. Nilanjan Ray](https://webdocs.cs.ualberta.ca/~nray1/) at the [VISUAL Laboratory](https://ualbertavisual.ca/).

My research sits at the intersection of **deep learning**, **computer vision**, and **real-world AI applications** — ranging from biomedical imaging to environmental monitoring and time-series forecasting.

## Research Focus

My current thesis work focuses on developing large-scale pretrained models for river streamflow prediction in snow-dominated and data-scarce environments. I am particularly interested in:

- **Attention-based architectures** for multimodal time-series data
- **Transfer learning and pretraining** strategies for scientific domains
- **Generative AI** for data augmentation in low-resource settings
- **Medical image analysis** — segmentation, detection, and synthesis

## Background

Before joining the University of Alberta, I completed my **Bachelor of Technology in Computer Science** from Heritage Institute of Technology, Kolkata (India), with a CGPA of 9.34/10.

During my undergraduate years I interned at:
- **Toronto Metropolitan University** — medical image analysis (MITACS Globalink)
- **UC Berkeley Future of Work Lab** — socioeconomic data analysis
- **Indian Statistical Institute** — satellite image analysis for environmental monitoring
